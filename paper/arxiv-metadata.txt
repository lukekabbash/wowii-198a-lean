Title: A Hamiltonian-Path Criterion from Bipartite Number and Average Eccentricity
Authors: Luke Kabbash
Subjects: Combinatorics (math.CO)
MSC classes: 05C45, 05C12
Comments: Includes a Lean 4 proof of the exact theorem, including the bound b(G) >= 2 rad(G), with no proof placeholders. The manuscript discloses an autonomous generative-AI research workflow and the limited scope of its prior-solution search; it makes no novelty or priority claim.
License: CC BY 4.0

Abstract:
For a finite nontrivial connected simple graph G, let b(G) be the
maximum order of an induced bipartite subgraph and let ebar(G) be the
average vertex eccentricity. We prove that b(G) <= 2 + ebar(G) implies
that G has a Hamiltonian path. This is the statement recorded as
Written on the Wall II, Conjecture 198a. The proof is driven by the
tight chain D+1 <= b(G) <= 2+ebar(G) <= D+2. In the first equality
case, every vertex outside a diametral path belongs to a clique layer
that can be inserted into the path. In the second, the graph is
self-centered of diameter two, and the Chvatal--Erdos traceability
theorem applies.
